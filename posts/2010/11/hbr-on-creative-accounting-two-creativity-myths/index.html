HBR: On Creative Accounting, Two Creativity Myths

November 29, 2010 1-minute read
Fun

Harvard Business Review had a good blog post this morning called On Creative Accounting: Two Creativity Myths. The term “creative accounting” conjures up images of Enron, and other massive financial reporting frauds. However, the author points out that creativity, itself, is morally neutral, and because creativity in accounting can be bad, that doesn’t mean that it must be bad. Also, I liked the cartoon.
